- **Regulatory Driver (PPT):** CT permit-driven model (MS4 GP + Industrial Stormwater GP + water-quality enforcement + release-based cleanup exposure).
- **How salt arrives (PPT):** Vessel-to-port logistics (project baseline assumption), then truck transfer to municipal use points.
- **Where bulk salt is handled (PPT):** Waterfront/port bulk handling is the baseline context under review.
- **Municipal storage location (PPT):** Day-to-day municipal storage pattern is being validated (split still under review).
- **Storage typology (PPT):** Current condition under review; target is covered storage with written SOP + inspections.

## Pattern synthesis

1. **Most defensible pattern:** keep marine logistics at port but keep regular municipal inventory inland in covered facilities.
2. **Controls hierarchy:** covered storage + impervious pad + managed drainage outperforms tarping-only approaches.
3. **Risk trend in CT:** enforcement risk increases when storage practices can trigger release-based cleanup duties, not just permit non-compliance.
